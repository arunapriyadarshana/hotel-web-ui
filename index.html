Room Types
Single Room

A single room is generally small and is intended for single occupancy. It will typically have one single-sized bed, although some single rooms are equipped with additional sleeping facilities or may have a second bed added if required. True single rooms are declining as hotels find it more efficient to offer twin rooms.

Double Room

A double room is intended for two people. Instead of two single beds, it will have a larger double bed intended for two people; this will typically be queen or king-sized. This type of room is best for couples (or people who don’t mind sharing a bed).

Cabana

A cabana is a type of hotel room that opens out onto a beach or a swimming pool. In some cases, the pool may be private and reserved only for the guests in that room. Cabanas are usually furnished in a luxurious style. Some have private balconies that allow beach access.

Deluxe Room

A duplex suite comprises two rooms on separate floors, with a private staircase or lift between them. A duplex is a good choice for a guest such as the business traveller who wants to work or hold meetings in one room and sleep in the other.
